India would be a little sceptical batting second on this. Moving on to the off-field celebrations that have been happening. The Prime Ministers meeting their respective teams and wishing them luck. The back drop for this game is huge!

"Shami scampering in to measure and mark his run-up now.... The players would have usually done this much before the toss. But today they've had to warmup on the B ground," quips Kaushik Rangarajan - who is present at the Narendra Modi Stadium..

Ravi Shastri is now having a conversation with the prime ministers as they go through a few memorable moments between India and Australia. Explains them the pictures of some significant events played between the two teams. Live action is not far away. Over to Kumar Abhisekh to take you through the first hour of this Test match...

Teams:

India (Playing XI): Rohit Sharma(c), Shubman Gill, Cheteshwar Pujara, Virat Kohli, Shreyas Iyer, Srikar Bharat(w), Ravindra Jadeja, Axar Patel, Ravichandran Ashwin, Mohammed Shami, Umesh Yadav

Australia (Playing XI): Travis Head, Usman Khawaja, Marnus Labuschagne, Steven Smith(c), Peter Handscomb, Cameron Green, Alex Carey(w), Mitchell Starc, Todd Murphy, Matthew Kuhnemann, Nathan Lyon

Rohit Sharma: We would have batted first. We know what needs to be done. Siraj is rested and Shami is back. It's always nice to have some time off. We need to regroup as a team, you can reflect on so many things. Not the surface what we saw in the first three Tests, looks a good pitch, I hope it stays the same for all five days.

Steve Smith: We are going to have a bat, playing with the same team. Looks like a nice surface, does look like a good wicket. The guys performed well last week. We'll wait and see (whether he will lead Australia in India again.)

Australia have won the toss and have opted to bat

Right then, it's toss time..

Right then, the prime ministers are presenting the Test caps to both the skippers. Mr. Modi presents the cap to Rohit Sharma, while Mr. Albanese presents the baggy green to Steve Smith. They'll have a lap of honour across the ground to celebrate 75 years of friendship between India and Australia.

The BCCI president (Roger Binny) is felicitating Australia's prime minister - Mr. Anthony Albanese, while the BCCI secretary (Jay Shah) felicitates the India prime minister - Mr. Narendra Modi. Some traditional music and dance to celebrate to follow next, as the crowd lets out a huge cheer to celebrate this occasion.

Pitch report: The pitch looks different to the one used against England. It looks well rolled, there is an even covering of grass and there are a few dry patches on the surface. Looks slightly damp as it's early in the morning. The spinners have loved to operate in these conditions. The surface is going to break up as the game progresses. Plenty of wickets in it for the spinners, but it will play better than the previous pitches used in the series, reckons Hayden.
